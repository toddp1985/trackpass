Best Texas Golf Membership 2026: TrackPass vs Private Club vs City Passes

Comparing your options as a Texas golfer who plays public courses regularly. What actually saves you money?

Published June 2026 · TrackPass

Texas has over 570 public golf courses and a golfer who plays 10+ rounds per year spends serious money at green fees alone. A Texas golf membership of some kind almost always makes financial sense. The question is which type: private club, city season pass, or a newer option like TrackPass that covers the whole state.

Option 1: Private Club Membership

Private club memberships in Texas typically run $5,000 to $30,000+ in initiation fees, plus $300 to $700/month in dues. That's a minimum of $8,600/year just to stay enrolled. For dedicated golfers who value the amenity, the locked tee times, and the club culture, it can be worth it. For anyone who wants flexibility to play different courses around the state, it is not.

Private club memberships tie you to one course. If you travel around Texas, play munis, or just like variety, you are paying initiation fees and dues for access you won't fully use.

Option 2: City/Municipal Season Pass

Most Texas cities with municipal courses offer resident season passes. Austin GolfATX charges roughly $600-900/year for unlimited play at Lions, Morris Williams, Roy Kizer, Jimmy Clay, and Hancock. Dallas city golf season passes run similar numbers. Houston's system has its own pricing structure.

City passes are great value if you live near those specific courses and play them heavily. The catch is that they only cover one city's courses. An Austin pass doesn't help you when you're visiting San Antonio, Houston, or Dallas.

Which Texas Golf Membership Is Right for You?

If you play the same two or three courses every week and live within 10 minutes of them, a city season pass likely makes more sense per round. If you play 10+ rounds per year across different courses, cities, or when traveling around Texas, TrackPass at $199 is the obvious choice -- the math is not close.

Private club membership is in a different category -- it's a lifestyle purchase, not a round-cost calculation. TrackPass doesn't compete with it. TrackPass is for golfers who want to play public courses across Texas without paying full green fee each time.
