Fort Worth Public Golf Courses 2026

Updated June 2026 • All 4 City-Operated Courses

Fort Worth has four city-operated public golf courses, all in the system. Three are traditional parkland layouts dating to the mid-20th century; one offers a hillier, open design. Green fees run $24–$45 — well below the national average for maintained public courses.

TrackPass members ($199/year flat rate) get 2 free rounds/year at partner courses, or green fees reimbursed up to $50/round (1 round/month, up to $199/year) at any other Texas public course.

Fort Worth City Courses

Pecan Valley Golf Club — River Course

$40/round

Pecan Valley's signature layout. Par 72, 7,100+ yards from the tips. Tree-lined fairways along the Trinity River bottom, bentgrass greens, and one of the longer public layouts in DFW. The River Course hosted the 1968 PGA Championship.

18 holes • Par 72 TrackPass: green fee reimbursed

Pecan Valley Golf Club — Hills Course

$40/round

More open and hillier than the River Course. Shorter at around 6,600 yards, playing par 72. Good option if you're looking for a faster round or a course where you can work on your irons — less punishing tree cover.

18 holes • Par 72 TrackPass: green fee reimbursed
View course details →

Rockwood Park Golf Course

$32/round

Classic west side muni in Rockwood Park near the Trinity River. Tight fairways, mature trees, and a reputation as one of the more challenging city courses for its length. Consistent pace of play makes it popular for weekday rounds.

18 holes • Par 71 TrackPass: green fee reimbursed
View course details →

Meadowbrook Golf Course

$24/round

Fort Worth's most affordable 18-hole course. Short, flat layout — approachable for beginners or high-handicappers, but don't be fooled by the price. The greens can be tricky and there are several tight tee shots on the back nine.

18 holes • Par 71 TrackPass: green fee reimbursed
